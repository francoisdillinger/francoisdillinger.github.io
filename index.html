About:

This is about a journey covering over 2000 miles of trail, through 14 different states from Georgia to Maine. Six months of sleeping under the stars, drinking from rivers, bathing in the rain, and missing one of lifes most overlooked pleasures: a toilet. An adventure of self-discovery, self-awareness, and self-indulgence. A story about setting out to chase a dream, and attempting to accomplish an impossible task in the process....finding Francois.

Here are some fun facts:

  • 1937:

    The year the Appalachian trail was finished, over 10 years after initial work on the trail began. While the trail's initial footpath was finished almost 80 years ago, work on it has never ceased. Today over 30 different trail clubs maintain different sections of this national treasure, and there all composed of volunteers!

  • 2190:

    Current lenght of the trail in miles. It changes slightly each year due to maintenance and re-routing in order to curb erosion and damage. It's estimated that almost the entire trail has been either relocated or rebuilt since 1937, so the trail is constantly changing.

  • 5000:

    The average number of calories a hiker burns each day. Though numbers can vary a bit depending on weight carried and how many miles a hiker covers each day. Since carrying 5000 calories worth of food would not only be impractical but extremely heavy, this calorie deficit is why its not uncommon for hikers to lose 30 lbs (or considerably more) during a "thru-hike".

  • 464,500:

    The estimated elevation gain/loss (in feet) that a hiker will cover during a hike of the entire trail. If you were to take the estimated elevation gain/loss and compare it to the tallest mountain in the world, it would be the equivalent of ascending Mount Everest 16 times! So you'd get all the work of climbing Everast several times, without the risk of Abominable Snowmen eating your food (just black bears).

  • 14:

    The number of states the Appalachian trail goes through. Georgia, North Carolina, Tennessee, Virginia, West Virginia, Maryland, Pennsylvania, New Jersey, New York, Connecticut, Massachusetts, Vermont, New Hampshire, and Maine. On average it takes between 4-6 months to complete. If you want to shorten that time you'll need to be an endurance athlete, skip sections, or do lines of cocaine for breakfast. (Just kidding!...I'd never encourage skipping sections!)

  • 16,000:

    My estimated number for how many hikers have successfully hiked the entire trail since its opening. The official number is slightly lower than this as of the year 2015 but there are hikers who do the trail but never register with the Appalachian Trail Conservancy. So I'll say 16,000. which sounds like alot, but really isnt when you consider thats since the trail officially opened almost 80 years ago.
